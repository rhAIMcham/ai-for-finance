Error What happens Financial reporting risk
Hallucination The AI generates information that is not in the source data. It may invent a figure, fabricate an explanation, or cite a standard that does not exist. Incorrect figures or fabricated content in reports could mislead decision-makers and breach reporting obligations.
Calculation errors The AI performs arithmetic incorrectly or applies the wrong formula. This is more common in complex or multi-step calculations. Incorrect totals, percentages or variances in financial reports.
Context gaps The AI does not know about events, decisions or circumstances not included in the data. It cannot account for a restructure, a new contract or a regulatory change it has not been told about. Commentary that misses material context, leading to incomplete or misleading narratives.
Outdated information The AI's training data has a cutoff date. It may reference accounting standards, tax rates or regulatory requirements that have since changed. Reports that reference incorrect or superseded regulatory requirements.
Bias and framing The AI may frame results more positively or negatively than warranted or emphasise certain items over others based on patterns in its training data rather than materiality. Management commentary that does not fairly represent the organisation's financial position.
Inconsistency Different prompts about the same data may produce different results. The AI may round figures differently, use different terminology, or frame the same variance differently each time. Inconsistent reporting across sections, periods or reports.
